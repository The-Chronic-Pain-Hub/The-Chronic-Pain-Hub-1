How it works

A geospatial recommender engine combines lived-experience signals with statewide access constraints. This module now pulls in Wisconsin shortage-area signals and pain-focused provider records to shape who gets prioritized first.

When Module 1 detects sleep issues and Module 2 shows high distress, the tool can favor clinics in lower-shortage regions, surface Medicaid-friendly pain specialists, and flag rural counties where telehealth or transportation support should be weighted more heavily.

Routing logic examples

  • Rural + high severity -> telehealth-first options
  • Medicaid -> ForwardHealth-accepting providers
  • High invalidation -> trauma-informed pain psychologist
  • Sleep cluster -> sleep clinic + CBT-I
  • Mobility barrier -> county transportation services
  • Language match -> culturally concordant providers

Output

Ranked care pathways plus non-pharmaceutical options such as PT, massage, acupuncture, and psychotherapy, now grounded in Wisconsin access and workforce data.
