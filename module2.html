Module 2

Visual Pain Expression System

Goal: bypass linguistic barriers by letting patients express pain through abstract animation categories.

Back to Module 1 Next: Bio-Acoustic

Animation taxonomy examples

  • Pulsing or throbbing
  • Flashing or stabbing
  • Radiating outward
  • Crushing pressure
  • Static tightness
  • Electric shock
  • Diffuse fog-like ache

How it works

Instead of asking patients to describe pain in words, the system offers a taxonomy of animated sensations. Patients choose what feels closest.

The AI maps each selection to a pain phenotype and compares it to linguistic tone to detect communication discordance.

AI role

  • Map animation selection -> structured pain descriptor
  • Correlate visual choices with spoken or written tone
  • Detect mismatch when language is mild but visuals are severe
  • Cluster cross-cultural visual patterns

Clinical signal

If a patient selects a high-intensity animation while using calm language, the system flags a high severity or symptom downplaying risk.

Research value

The visual taxonomy becomes a consistent, analyzable data layer across languages and cultures.
