Module 1

Semantic Translator and Pre-Visit Builder

Goal: convert cultural metaphors and native-language narratives into medical ontologies that clinicians can act on.

Next: Visual Expression Jump to Bio-Acoustic

Primary outputs

  • One-page visit summary
  • Pain phenotype profile
  • Top concerns and key questions
  • Optional emotional context flags

How it works

The translator goes beyond literal translation. It uses cross-lingual NLU to map metaphors and narratives into structured clinical descriptors.

Example: if a patient says "pain like a ghost in the marrow," the AI generates a clinician-facing note: "Patient describes deep, non-localized somatic distress (McGill Category: Affective/Exhausting)."

Core functions

  • Write or speak in preferred language
  • Guided prompts with adaptive questioning
  • Location selection using a body map
  • Animation-based pain quality selection
  • Context prompts for sleep, stress, food, flare pattern

AI layer

  • Cross-lingual semantic mapping (not literal translation)
  • Metaphor detection and clarification prompts
  • Structured clinical summary generation

Problem fit

  • Hard to think of responses during visits
  • Translation loses meaning or intent
  • Numbers do not translate pain
  • Symptoms are misinterpreted
